Building a tool for ops teams to manage events and crises

The opportunity

During crises and events where streets or areas may be closed, we need to restrict orders and dispatches. In the past, our operations teams used a variety of legacy tools to create restricted walls and zones. The experience was manual, time-consuming and error-prone. Walls were permanent, couldn't be scheduled, took a long time to create, and inflexible (e.g. you couldn't block cars but allow bikes).

Defining and designing the new tool

From the start, I included our operations teams in our design reviews. This helped us validate and refine early assumptions about product priorities. For example, our product team had previous believed that viewing all live walls on a map was a requirement for launch but we instead discovered that it was more impactful to improve the flexibility of walls (e.g. excluding vehicle and restaurant types from walls) and enable bulk creation. In the end, we launched our new tool in time for the holidays and improved team efficiency as well as marketplace reliability.

Walls and areas were drawn in an existing tool and imported.

We allowed users to create both temporary and permanent walls. We also allowed ops teams to set complex rules for walls.

Live and upcoming walls are displayed in a filterable table.

Walls are viewable by all users but only editable by users with permissions.

Users are also able to bulk upload walls from a csv.
